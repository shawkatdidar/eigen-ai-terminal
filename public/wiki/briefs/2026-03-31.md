## Today I Learned

### Investment-Grade GPU Debt: A New Category of AI Financing

**Why this came up:** CoreWeave's $8.5B loan achieved A3 rating from Moody's — the first GPU-backed debt to reach investment grade.

Until now, AI infrastructure was funded through venture capital (equity rounds where founders give up ownership) or unrated private debt (loans that don't have a credit rating, limiting who can invest). CoreWeave's deal changes this by demonstrating that GPUs + a customer contract from a creditworthy company (Meta, rated investment-grade itself) can back investment-grade bonds.

Why this matters mechanically: investment-grade ratings determine which institutional investors are legally allowed to buy the debt. Pension funds, insurance companies, and sovereign wealth funds typically have mandates that require investment-grade assets. Before this deal, those institutions — which collectively manage tens of trillions of dollars — couldn't invest in AI compute infrastructure. Now they can.

The borrowing cost difference is significant: CoreWeave's rate was SOFR + 2.25% (~7.5% total). Unrated or junk-rated GPU loans might cost SOFR + 5-8% (~10-13%). That 3-5 percentage point spread, on billions of dollars, translates to hundreds of millions in annual interest savings — capital that can be redirected into more GPUs.

### Silicon Photonics: Why NVIDIA Invested $2B in Light-Based Interconnects

**Why this came up:** NVIDIA's $2B Marvell investment specifically targets silicon photonics technology.

In a modern AI datacenter, thousands of GPUs need to communicate with each other during both training and inference. Currently, this communication happens over copper wiring (electrical signals). The problem: copper generates heat proportional to the data rate, and at the bandwidths AI workloads demand, thermal management becomes a major engineering challenge and cost center.

Silicon photonics replaces electrical signals with light pulses traveling through silicon waveguides (microscopic channels etched into silicon that guide photons the way fiber optic cables do, but at chip scale). Light signals don't generate heat proportional to bandwidth, consume less power, and can carry more data per second.

Marvell acquired Celestial AI specifically for their "photonic fabric" technology. NVIDIA's $2B investment + NVLink Fusion integration means this technology will be integrated directly into NVIDIA's AI factory stack — not as an add-on, but as a core component of how GPUs talk to each other.

### Quick Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Investment-grade | Credit rating indicating low default risk (BBB-/Baa3 or higher). Opens access to institutional investors with conservative mandates. |
| SOFR | Secured Overnight Financing Rate — the benchmark interest rate for US dollar-denominated loans. Currently ~5.3%. |
| NVLink Fusion | NVIDIA's high-speed interconnect protocol that lets different chip types (GPUs, custom XPUs) communicate directly. |
| Silicon photonics | Technology using light pulses through silicon waveguides for chip-to-chip data transfer, replacing copper wiring. |
| Source map | A debugging file that maps minified/compiled production code back to original source code. Not meant for public distribution. |
| XPU | Custom-designed AI accelerator chip built for specific workloads (as opposed to general-purpose GPUs). |
| RAT | Remote Access Trojan — malware that gives an attacker persistent remote control of a compromised system. |
