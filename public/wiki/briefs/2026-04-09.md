## Today I Learned

### The Latent Planning Ceiling
**Why this came up:** The Depth Ceiling paper (ArXiv 2604.06427) discovered that LLMs have a hard limit on how many reasoning steps they can perform internally — without writing out their thinking.

When an LLM processes a prompt, it passes through multiple layers of computation (each layer is one "step" of processing). The Depth Ceiling research measured how many of these steps can actually perform useful reasoning:

- **Tiny transformers:** max 3 latent steps
- **Fine-tuned GPT-4o / Qwen3-32B:** reach 5 steps
- **GPT-5.4 with few-shot prompting:** reaches 7 steps

This explains a practical puzzle: why does "chain-of-thought" prompting (asking the model to show its work) dramatically improve performance? Because it **bypasses the ceiling** — instead of doing all reasoning in the model's internal layers (limited to 3-7 steps), the model writes intermediate results to the output, which then get fed back as new input, effectively giving it unlimited reasoning depth.

An important nuance: models can **discover** strategies at 5 steps but **execute** them out to 8 steps. The bottleneck is finding the approach, not applying it.

### Cross-Model Capability Transfer
**Why this came up:** The Master Key Hypothesis paper (ArXiv 2604.06377) showed you can extract a trained capability from one model and inject it into another.

When a model is post-trained to gain a capability (like mathematical reasoning), the change is encoded as a direction in a high-dimensional vector space — the model's internal representation space. The UNLOCK framework identifies this "capability direction" using linear subspace alignment, then applies it to a different model. A 12.1% accuracy gain on MATH was achieved by transplanting reasoning from a 14B model into a 7B model — without retraining the 7B model at all.

### Quick Glossary
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Latent planning | Reasoning that happens inside the model's layers without being written to output |
| Chain-of-thought (CoT) | Prompting technique where the model writes out intermediate reasoning steps |
| Linear subspace alignment | Mathematical technique for finding corresponding directions in two different vector spaces |
| Template collapse | Failure mode where RL-trained agents produce outputs that look varied but are actually input-agnostic patterns |
| Tensor parallelism | Splitting individual matrix operations across multiple GPUs simultaneously (vs pipeline parallelism which splits layers) |
| IGV ETF | iShares Expanded Tech-Software Sector ETF — a proxy for SaaS stock health |
