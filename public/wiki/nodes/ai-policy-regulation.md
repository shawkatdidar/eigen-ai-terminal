# AI Policy & Regulation

## Current State

AI regulation is entering a turbulent new phase. In the US, the Anthropic-Pentagon confrontation is reshaping the landscape: Anthropic has sued the Pentagon over a supply-chain risk designation, while DOD officials publicly characterize Anthropic's safety "red lines" as a national security risk. Senator Blackburn's TRUMP AMERICA AI Act draft proposes comprehensive federal preemption of state-level AI regulation. NIST has entered an active engagement phase on AI Agent Standards, reflecting the urgency of governing autonomous systems.

Internationally, China has restricted state enterprises from using OpenClaw over security concerns, mirroring US-China decoupling dynamics. State-level activity continues with Washington's HB 1170 AI transparency bill sent to the governor.

Key tensions: AI safety commitments vs. national security demands, federal vs. state regulatory authority, agentic AI governance gaps, and bilateral security restrictions fragmenting the global AI ecosystem.
