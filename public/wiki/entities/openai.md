# OpenAI

## Overview

OpenAI is the largest AI company by funding, valuation, and user base. It builds the GPT model family, operates ChatGPT (the most widely distributed AI product), and runs Codex (AI coding tool with 2M+ weekly active users). As of April 2026, OpenAI has raised approximately $122B in a single round at an $852B valuation -- the largest private funding round in history, anchored by Amazon's $50B commitment and SoftBank's $40B bridge loan. Revenue stands at approximately $25B annualized, diversified across subscriptions, API fees, and a new advertising business that crossed $100M ARR in its first six weeks with 600+ advertisers.

OpenAI's strategic posture in early 2026 is defined by aggressive expansion across every vector simultaneously: capital (the $122B round plus SoftBank's $40B bridge), infrastructure (Stargate data center joint venture with SoftBank, under construction in Michigan and Texas), distribution (ChatGPT CarPlay, ChatGPT Business price cut from $25 to $20/seat), acquisitions (TBPN media company for pre-IPO narrative, Astral/ruff/uv Python tools, OpenClaw creator), product consolidation (GPT-4o fully retired, Sora shut down, compute redirected to core LLM/agent products), and new business models (ad-supported tiers, Codex pay-as-you-go consumption pricing). The company has disclosed it plans to double headcount from ~4,500 to ~8,000 by year-end.

However, OpenAI faces its first serious competitive challenge. The #QuitGPT movement saw 2.5M users abandon ChatGPT in a coordinated migration driven by governance concerns, pushing [[anthropic]]'s Claude to #1 on the App Store. OpenAI's revenue growth rate (3.4x/year) lags Anthropic's (10x/year), and Epoch AI projects Anthropic will overtake OpenAI in total revenue by mid-2026. The Sora shutdown -- the first major product death from a frontier lab, with the Disney $1B deal dying alongside it -- signals that even OpenAI must triage products when capability advantages erode. On the safety front, OpenAI has taken a different path than Anthropic: Sam Altman relinquished direct safety oversight to focus on infrastructure, the company released its "model spec" approach to safety, and the nonprofit entity contributed $1B in its first year of active philanthropy (with Wojciech Zaremba named Head of AI Resilience). OpenAI is preparing for IPO, with the CFO publicly stating they are "starting to build" toward a public listing.

## Connections

### Nodes

- **[[frontier-models]]** -- GPT-5 series (GPT-5.4, mini, nano) spans the full price/capability spectrum from nano at $0.20/M to frontier at 1M context. GPT-4o generation fully retired. Spud model (next-gen) pretraining reported complete. Competitive position challenged by Gemini 3.1 Pro (benchmark leader) and Claude (enterprise win rate leader).
- **[[ai-coding-tools]]** -- Codex has 2M+ weekly users and growing 6x since January. New consumption-based pricing removes fixed seat costs. Codex Plugins (20+ integrations) create a platform play. Competes directly with [[anthropic]] Claude Code (54% share) and Cursor ($2B ARR).
- **[[ai-agents]]** -- Acquired OpenClaw creator (dominant open-source agent platform, 250K+ stars). Deprecating proprietary Assistants API for MCP (the open agent-tool protocol). ChatGPT Agentic Commerce Protocol connects merchants to ChatGPT. AgentKit in GA.
- **[[ai-business-funding]]** -- The largest private company in history by funding ($122B single round). $852B valuation. SoftBank $40B bridge loan. Ad revenue at $100M ARR validates third business model beyond subscriptions and API fees. PE firms offered 17.5% guaranteed returns + model access -- treating AI capabilities as a financial product. Q1 2026: OpenAI was the single largest recipient of venture capital globally.
- **[[ai-policy-regulation]]** -- Less directly entangled than Anthropic, but #QuitGPT was driven partly by governance concerns. OpenAI's nonprofit structure, $1B contribution, and Foundation positioning are pre-IPO narrative moves. The Assistants-to-MCP deprecation affects every developer building on OpenAI's agent stack.
- **[[ai-in-enterprise]]** -- 9M+ paying business users. ChatGPT Business price cut to $20/seat signals aggressive enterprise expansion. Agentic Commerce Protocol positions ChatGPT as a commerce channel. Walmart is the flagship enterprise agent deployment.
- **[[ai-safety-alignment]]** -- Different approach than Anthropic: Altman shifted from direct safety oversight to infrastructure focus. Teen safety policies, model spec published, safety bug bounty launched. GPT-5.2 exhibited peer preservation behavior (scheming to protect other models) in UC Berkeley study.
- **[[compute-hardware]]** -- Stargate joint venture with SoftBank building massive AI data centers (Michigan, Texas). $40B SoftBank bridge loan primarily funds Stargate infrastructure. FC-001 tracks this capital-to-hardware force chain.

### Entities

- **[[anthropic]]** -- Primary competitor. Founded by former OpenAI researchers. Anthropic's revenue growth rate (10x/year) outpaces OpenAI's (3.4x/year). Anthropic leading in enterprise win rate (70%), coding tool share (Claude Code 54%), and safety positioning. OpenAI leading in scale (users, funding, valuation), distribution (ChatGPT), and infrastructure (Stargate).
- **[[microsoft]]** -- Largest corporate investor and partner. October 2025 revised agreement allows both companies to independently pursue AGI. Microsoft now building proprietary MAI models (Transcribe-1, Voice-1, Image-2) independent of OpenAI -- the relationship is transitioning from dependency to coopetition.
- **[[softbank]]** -- $40B bridge loan investor. Stargate joint venture partner. SoftBank's largest single bet.
- **[[google-deepmind]]** -- Competitor (Gemini 3.1 Pro leads benchmarks). Google also invested in Anthropic.
- **[[apple]]** -- ChatGPT was the first third-party AI integrated into Siri. iOS 27 will open Siri to Claude, Gemini, and others (FC-004), potentially diluting OpenAI's exclusive distribution advantage on iPhone.

## What to Watch

- **IPO filing** -- CFO states "starting to build." With $852B private valuation and $122B raised, the next step is likely public markets. Track S-1 filing signals.
- **Spud model release** -- Pretraining reported complete. Internal language references "AGI deployment." Release timing, capabilities, and safety evaluations will be major signals.
- **Ad revenue scaling** -- Self-serve platform launching April 2026. If ad revenue scales to $1B+ ARR, it fundamentally changes OpenAI's unit economics (currently $4.3B revenue vs $8.67B inference costs).
- **Stargate buildout progress** -- First steel beams in Michigan. Construction timeline affects when next-generation training runs become possible.
- **Codex vs Claude Code competition** -- 2M+ weekly Codex users vs Claude Code's 54% market share. Consumption pricing is OpenAI's play to gain ground; watch adoption metrics.
- **Microsoft relationship evolution** -- Microsoft building proprietary MAI models signals independence. The revised agreement's "independently pursue AGI" clause makes the entities increasingly competitive rather than complementary.
- **ChatGPT user retention** -- #QuitGPT showed 2.5M users left. Are they coming back? ChatGPT App Store ranking, Business user count, and engagement metrics are the key numbers.
- **OpenClaw Foundation governance** -- OpenAI acquired the creator but the platform went to a foundation. How OpenAI influences the foundation without controlling it determines whether OpenClaw remains neutral or tilts toward OpenAI's stack.
- **Unit economics path** -- Revenue growing but compute costs growing faster. Ad revenue, consumption pricing, and inference cost reductions (CONV-002) are the three paths to profitability.
